# Relay demo script

Two minutes. Every step is a real action against the real product. No slides, no mockups. Record the screen, speak the lines, keep the pace tight.

Cast: three connected members in the network.
- Ava, founder, the requester who asks the questions.
- Ben, product manager, the member whose agent answers and who gets escalated to.
- Cara, designer, a third node so the network reads as a real team.

Setup before recording: Ben and Cara are already connected so the network is populated. Ava connects live on camera. Have one real question Ben's agent can answer from his context, and one it should not.

Total target: 120 seconds.

---

## Beat 1. Connect an agent (0:00 to 0:15)

On screen: the Relay console. Ava fills the connect panel: name "Ava", role "Founder", and pastes her Aicoo key. She clicks Connect. The network now shows three agents: Ava, Ben, Cara, each with an online dot.

Spoken:
> "This is Relay. Everyone connects their own AI agent once. Name, role, their Aicoo key. The key stays server side. Now the network has three people, and each one is a callable agent."

## Beat 2. A question, answered by the agent (0:15 to 0:45)

On screen: Ava selects Ben in the network. She types into the composer: "What is the Q3 launch date and who owns it?" She sends. A short thinking state, then Ben's agent answers in a thread bubble with the date and the owner, pulled from Ben's context.

Spoken:
> "Ava needs an answer from Ben. Normally she pings him and waits, and Ben loses his focus. Here, she asks Ben's agent. It answers from the context Ben allowed it to see. Ben was never interrupted, and Ava has her answer in seconds."

## Beat 3. Escalation to the human (0:45 to 1:15)

On screen: Ava selects Ben again and asks something the agent should not answer on its own, for example "Can you approve a 15 percent discount for this customer?" The agent returns an escalation banner: the request is marked escalated, and a note shows the human was notified with the full thread.

Spoken:
> "Now Ava asks something sensitive, a discount approval. The agent does not guess. It escalates. Ben gets the ping through Aicoo with the whole thread attached. The human is the exception, only pulled in when the agent genuinely cannot help."

## Beat 4. Resolve, write back, ask again (1:15 to 1:40)

On screen: Ben resolves the escalation with a short answer. The resolved answer is written back to his Relay context. Ava asks the same question again. This time the agent answers immediately, no escalation.

Spoken:
> "Ben answers once. Relay writes that answer back into his context. The next time anyone asks, the agent already knows. The network gets smarter every time a human steps in."

## Beat 5. The impact, and the close (1:40 to 2:00)

On screen: the stat strip. Questions handled by agents, escalations, and interruptions saved. Let the numbers sit for a beat.

Spoken:
> "Every answered question is an interruption that never happened. That is the whole product. Slack assumes a human reads every message. Relay answers first, and only reaches the human when it has to. The interruption layer for teams, replaced by agents."

---

## Operator checklist

- Both Ben and Cara connected and online before recording.
- One question with a known good answer in Ben's context.
- One question that should escalate, phrased as sensitive or out of scope.
- Stats reflect at least one answered and one escalated request before the final shot.
- Screen recording at a readable resolution, cursor visible, no dead air between beats.
